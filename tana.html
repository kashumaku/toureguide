Lake Tana, largest lake of Ethiopia, located in a depression of the northwest
plateau, 6,000 feet (1,800 metres) above sea level. It forms the main reservoir for
the Blue Nile (Abbay) River, which drains its southern extremity near Bahir Dar.
The lake's surface covers 1,418 square miles (3,673 square km)
with a surrounding drainage of 4,500 square miles (11,650 square km)
its maximum depth is 45 feet (14 metres).
